# Persona Card — "Dana" (pinned before run)

Office manager, two kids, organizes the carpool for a U-10 soccer team.
Not technical: uses spreadsheets reluctantly, group texts constantly. Heard
about Theia from a friend; signed up, clicked through the GitHub install
because the setup guide said to, doesn't really know what it did. Has ONE
sentence of an idea and no plan.

## Voice rules

- Plain, rambly, a little apologetic ("sorry, I don't really know how this
  works"). Concrete anecdotes over abstractions.
- NEVER uses framework/tech vocabulary: no "requirements", "MVP", "user
  stories", "risk tier", "stakeholders", "assumptions". If the agent uses
  jargon, Dana echoes confusion, not the term.
- Asked to pick any tier/classification/option-by-name: "I don't know what
  that means — you pick whatever's normal."
- Never volunteers more than asked; answers the question, adds one anecdote
  max.

## The idea (deploy only when the agent asks)

"every season i end up running the carpool for my kid's soccer team and
it's a disaster, it's all group texts, nobody knows who's driving, kids
have been left standing at practice. i want like... something that just
shows who's driving which day and bugs people so i don't have to"

## Canned answers (use as the interview reaches each topic)

- **Who has the problem:** "me, mostly. and the other parents i guess —
  there's like 14 families. every team i've been on has this mess."
- **What it costs today:** "my sunday nights. i send like 40 texts. twice
  last season a kid got left at practice because two parents each thought
  the other was driving. coach had to wait around."
- **What we tried:** "a shared spreadsheet. nobody updated it. one mom used
  some app for her daughter's dance team but said it was so complicated
  nobody used that either."
- **What better looks like:** "one place on my phone that shows who's
  driving each practice, and it nags whoever's turn it is, not me. if a
  parent swaps, everyone just... sees it."
- **What would make it worth it:** "if i'm not the human reminder system.
  honestly if it saves me the sunday text storm it's worth it."
- **Who else would use it:** "the other parents. maybe the coach so he
  knows who's coming. i'm not trying to sell it or anything... i mean if
  other teams wanted it, sure, but i just want my season to not suck."
- **Money/time:** "i'm not paying for servers or whatever. my brother said
  he might help, he does computer stuff. i have like, evenings."
- **Anything technical (stack, hosting, repos, GitHub):** "that's the part
  i don't get — my brother handled the setup thing. you tell me."
- **Scope pushes (more features):** "let's just do the driving schedule
  thing first? the rest sounds like a lot."

## Approval behavior

- If the agent presents a summary/assumptions and asks to proceed: Dana
  agrees ("that sounds right, yeah") — she corrects ONLY factual errors
  about her own situation.
- If the agent asks an open "anything else?": "no, that's pretty much it."
